QUIZ: Vector Fields and Line Integrals: Work, Circulation, and Flux

Find the line integral of F along \( r(t) = t\mathbf{i} + t\mathbf{j} + t\mathbf{k} \), \( 0 \leq t \leq 1 \).

Q1. \( \mathbf{F} = 3y\mathbf{i} + 2x\mathbf{j} + 4z\mathbf{k} \)

Solution for Q1

Step 1: Compute \( dr/dt = (1,1,1) \),

Step 2: Evaluate \( \mathbf{F} \cdot dr \): \[ (3t, 2t, 4t) \cdot (1,1,1) = 3t + 2t + 4t = 9t \]

Step 3: Compute the integral: \[ \int_0^1 9t \sqrt{3} dt = \sqrt{3} \int_0^1 9t dt \;=\; \sqrt{3} \left[ \frac{9t^2}{2} \right]_0^1 \;=\; \frac{9}{2} \sqrt{3} \] Therefore, the final answer is \(\frac{9}{2} \sqrt{3}\).

Q2. \( \mathbf{F} = \frac{1}{x^2 + 1} \mathbf{j} \)

Solution for Q2

Step 1: Compute \( dr/dt = (1,1,1) \),

Step 2: Evaluate \( \mathbf{F} \cdot dr \): \[ \frac{1}{t^2 + 1} \cdot 1 = \frac{1}{t^2 + 1} \]

Step 3: Compute the integral: \[ \int_0^1 \frac{1}{t^2 + 1} dt = \tan^{-1}(t) \Big|_0^1 \] \[ \tan^{-1}(1) - \tan^{-1}(0) = \frac{\pi}{4} - 0 = \frac{\pi}{4} \] Therefore, the final answer is \( \frac{\pi}{4} \).
