CALCULUS II

QUIZ: Tangential and Normal Components of Acceleration

Write the acceleration \( \mathbf{a} \) in the form \( \mathbf{a} = a_T T + a_N N \) without finding \( T \) and \( N \) (find \( a_T \) and \( a_N \)).

Q1. \( \mathbf{r}(t) = a \cos t \hat{i} + a \sin t \hat{j} + b t \hat{k} \)

Solution for Q1

The position vector is \( \mathbf{r}(t) = a \cos t \hat{i} + a \sin t \hat{j} + b t \hat{k} \).

The velocity is: \[ \mathbf{v}(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(a \cos t \hat{i} + a \sin t \hat{j} + b t \hat{k}) = -a \sin t \hat{i} + a \cos t \hat{j} + b \hat{k}. \]

The acceleration is: \[ \mathbf{a}(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(-a \sin t \hat{i} + a \cos t \hat{j} + b \hat{k}) = -a \cos t \hat{i} - a \sin t \hat{j}. \]

The tangential acceleration \( a_T \) is: \[ a_T = \frac{\mathbf{a}(t) \cdot \mathbf{v}(t)}{|\mathbf{v}(t)|} = -a \sin t \cdot a \cos t + a \cos t \cdot a \sin t = -a \sin t \cdot a \cos t + a \cos t \cdot a \sin t = 0. \]

The normal acceleration \( a_N \) is: \[ a_N = \frac{|\mathbf{a}(t) \times \mathbf{v}(t)|}{|\mathbf{v}(t)|} = a. \]

So, the correct answer is: \( a_T = 0 \) and \( a_N = a \).

Q2. \( \mathbf{r}(t) = 3 \sin t \hat{i} + 3 \cos t \hat{j} + 4 t \hat{k} \)

Solution for Q2

The position vector is \( \mathbf{r}(t) = 3 \sin t \hat{i} + 3 \cos t \hat{j} + 4 t \hat{k} \).

The velocity is: \[ \mathbf{v}(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(3 \sin t \hat{i} + 3 \cos t \hat{j} + 4 t \hat{k}) = 3 \cos t \hat{i} - 3 \sin t \hat{j} + 4 \hat{k}. \]

The magnitude of the velocity is: \[ |\mathbf{v}(t)| = \sqrt{(3 \cos t)^2 + (-3 \sin t)^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 \cos^2 t + 9 \sin^2 t + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5. \]

The unit tangent vector is: \[ T = \frac{\mathbf{v}(t)}{|\mathbf{v}(t)|} = \frac{1}{5} (3 \cos t \hat{i} - 3 \sin t \hat{j} + 4 \hat{k}). \]

The second derivative is: \[ \mathbf{a}(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(3 \cos t \hat{i} - 3 \sin t \hat{j} + 4 \hat{k}) = -3 \sin t \hat{i} - 3 \cos t \hat{j}. \]

The normal vector is: \[ N = \frac{\mathbf{a}(t)}{|\mathbf{a}(t)|} = - \sin t \hat{i} - \cos t \hat{j}. \]

The binormal vector is: \[ B = T \times N. \] \[ B = -\frac{1}{5} (4 \cos t \hat{i} - 4 \sin t \hat{j} - \hat{k}). \]

The curvature \( k \) and torsion \( t \) can be calculated from the second derivative of the position and the cross-product formulas for \( B \).

So, the correct answer is the first one.
