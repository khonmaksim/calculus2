QUIZ: Line integrals of Scalar Functions

Evaluating Line Integrals over Space Curves.

Q1. \( \int_C (x + y) ds \) where \( C \) is the straight-line segment \( x = t, y = (1 - t), z = 0 \), from (0,1,0) to (1,0,0).

Solution for Q1

Step 1: Parameterize the curve \( C \) as \( r(t) = (t, 1 - t, 0) \), where \( 0 \leq t \leq 1 \).

Step 2: Compute \( ds \): \[ ds = \sqrt{(dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2 + (dz/dt)^2} dt = \sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2 + 0^2} dt = \sqrt{2} dt \].

Step 3: Evaluate the integral: \[ \int_0^1 (t + (1 - t)) \sqrt{2} dt \] \[ \int_0^1 1 \cdot \sqrt{2} dt = \sqrt{2} \]

Q2. \( \int_C (x - y + z - 2) ds \) where \( C \) is the straight-line segment \( x = t, y = (1 - t), z = 1 \), from (0,1,1) to (1,0,1).

Solution for Q2

Step 1: Parameterize the curve \( C \) as \( r(t) = (t, 1 - t, 1) \), where \( 0 \leq t \leq 1 \).

Step 2: Compute ds: \[ ds = \sqrt{(dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2 + (dz/dt)^2} dt = \sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2 + 0^2} dt = \sqrt{2} dt .\]

Step 3: Evaluate the integral: \[ \int_0^1 (t - (1 - t) + 1 - 2) \sqrt{2} dt \;=\; \int_0^1 (t - 1 + t + 1 - 2) \sqrt{2} dt \;=\; \int_0^1 (2t - 2) \sqrt{2} dt \] \[ \sqrt{2} \int_0^1 (2t - 2) dt \;=\; \sqrt{2} \left[ t^2 - 2t \right]_0^1 \;=\; \sqrt{2} (1 - 2 - (0 - 0)) \;=\; \sqrt{2} (-1) = -\sqrt{2} \]

Q3. \( \int_C (xy + y + z) ds \) along the curve \( r(t) = 2ti + tj + (2 - 2t)k \), \( 0 \leq t \leq 1 \).

Solution for Q3

The partial derivatives are:

Step 1: Parameterize the curve \( r(t) = (2t, t, 2 - 2t) \) and compute \( ds \).

Step 2: Compute \( ds = \sqrt{(dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2 + (dz/dt)^2} dt = \sqrt{4 + 1 + 4} dt = 3 dt \).

Step 3: Evaluate the integral: \[ \int_0^1 (2t \cdot t + t + (2 - 2t)) 3 dt \] \[ \int_0^1 (2t^2 + t + 2 - 2t) 3 dt \] \[ \int_0^1 (2t^2 - t + 2) 3 dt = \frac{13}{2}\]
