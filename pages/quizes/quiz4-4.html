CALCULUS II

QUIZ: Surfaces and Area

Find a parametrization of the surface. (There are many correct ways to do these)

Q1. \( z = x^2 + y^2, \quad z \leq 4 \)

Solution for Q1

The given surface is a paraboloid described by \( z = x^2 + y^2 \). Using polar coordinates:

Set \( x = r \cos \theta \), \( y = r \sin \theta \), then \( z = r^2 \). Since \( z \leq 4 \), we have \( r^2 \leq 4 \Rightarrow 0 \leq r \leq 2 \).

The complete parametrization is: \[ x = r \cos \theta, \quad y = r \sin \theta, \quad z = r^2, \quad 0 \leq r \leq 2, \quad 0 \leq \theta < 2\pi \]

Q2. \( z = 9 - x^2 - y^2, \quad z \geq 0 \)

Solution for Q2

The given surface is a downward-facing paraboloid described by \( z = 9 - x^2 - y^2 \). Using polar coordinates:

Set \( x = r \cos \theta \), \( y = r \sin \theta \), then \( z = 9 - r^2 \). Since \( z \geq 0 \), we have \( 9 - r^2 \geq 0 \Rightarrow r^2 \leq 9 \Rightarrow 0 \leq r \leq 3 \).

The complete parametrization is: \[ x = r \cos \theta, \quad y = r \sin \theta, \quad z = 9 - r^2, \quad 0 \leq r \leq 3, \quad 0 \leq \theta < 2\pi \]
