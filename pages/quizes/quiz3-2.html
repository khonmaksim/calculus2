QUIZ: Double Integrals over General Regions

Evaluate the integral.

Q1. \( \int_{1}^{\ln 8} \int_{0}^{\ln y} e^{x+y} \ dx \ dy \)

Solution for Q1

Step 1: Compute the inner integral: \[ \int_{0}^{\ln y} e^{x+y} dx = e^y \int_{0}^{\ln y} e^x dx \] \[ = e^y \cdot e^x \Big|_{0}^{\ln y} = e^y (e^{\ln y} - 1) = e^y (y - 1) \] Step 2: Compute the outer integral: \[ \int_{1}^{\ln 8} e^y (y - 1) dy = 24\ln \left(2\right)-16+e\]

Q2. \( \int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{y^2} 3y^3 e^{xy} \ dx \ dy \)

Solution for Q2

Step 1: Compute the inner integral: \[ \int_{0}^{y^2} 3y^3 e^{xy} dx \;=\; 3y^3 \int_{0}^{y^2} e^{xy} dx \;=\; \frac{e^{xy}}{y} \Big|_0^{y^2} \;=\; \frac{e^{y^3} - 1}{y} \]

Step 2: Compute the outer integral: \[ \int_{0}^{1} 3y^2 (e^{y^3} - 1) dy \;=\; 3 \int_{0}^{1} y^2 e^{y^3} dy - 3 \int_{0}^{1} y^2 dy \] Substituting \( u = y^3 \), so \( du = 3y^2 dy \), transforms the first integral: \[ \int_{0}^{1} y^2 e^{y^3} dy = \frac{1}{3} \int_{0}^{1} e^u du = \frac{1}{3} (e^1 - e^0) = \frac{1}{3} (e - 1) \] The second integral evaluates as: \[ \int_{0}^{1} y^2 dy = \frac{1}{3} \] So the full result is: \[ 3 \left( \frac{1}{3} (e - 1) \right) - 3 \times \frac{1}{3} \] \[ = (e - 1) - 1 = e - 2 \]

Q3. \( \int_{0}^{\pi} \int_{0}^{x} x \sin y \ dy \ dx \)

Solution for Q3

The partial derivatives are:

Step 1: Compute the inner integral: \[ \int_{0}^{x} x \sin y dy = x (-\cos y) \Big|_{0}^{x} = x(-\cos x + 1) \] Step 2: Compute the outer integral: \[ \int_{0}^{\pi} x(1 - \cos x) dx \;=\; \pi ^2/2\]
