QUIZ: Tangent Planes and Differentials

Find the equations for the tangent plane and normal line for the surfaces

Q1. \( x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 3 \) at the point \( P_0(1, 1, 1) \).

Solution for Q1

The equation of the tangent plane is given by:

The gradient of \( F(x, y, z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 3 \) at the point \( P_0(1, 1, 1) \) is: \( \nabla F = \langle 2x, 2y, 2z \rangle = \langle 2, 2, 2 \rangle \).

The equation of the tangent plane is: \[ 2(x - 1) + 2(y - 1) + 2(z - 1) = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2x + 2y + 2z = 6. \] The normal line is: \[ x = 1 + t, \, y = 1 + t, \, z = 1 + t. \]

So, the correct option is the first one.

Q2. \( x^2 + y^2 - z^2 = 18 \) at the point \( P_0(3, 5, -4) \).

Solution for Q2

The equation of the tangent plane is given by:

The gradient of \( F(x, y, z) = x^2 + y^2 - z^2 - 18 \) at the point \( P_0(3, 5, -4) \) is: \( \nabla F = \langle 2x, 2y, -2z \rangle = \langle 6, 10, 8 \rangle \).

The equation of the tangent plane is: \[ 6(x - 3) + 10(y - 5) + 8(z + 4) = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2x + 2y - 2z = 36. \] The normal line is: \[ x = 3 + t, \, y = 5 + 2t, \, z = -4 - 3t. \]

So, the correct option is the second one.

Q3. \( 2z - x^2 = 0 \) at the point \( P_0(2, 0, 2) \).

Solution for Q3

The equation of the tangent plane is given by:

The gradient of \( F(x, y, z) = 2z - x^2 \) at the point \( P_0(2, 0, 2) \) is: \( \nabla F = \langle -2x, 0, 2 \rangle = \langle -4, 0, 2 \rangle \).

The equation of the tangent plane is: \[ -4(x - 2) + 0(y - 0) + 2(z - 2) = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad -4x + 2z = -4. \] The normal line is: \[ x = 2 + t, \, y = 0 + t, \, z = 2 - t. \]

So, the correct option is the first one.
