QUIZ: Partial Derivatives

Q1. Find the gradient of the function \( f(x, y) = y - x \) at the point \( (2, 1) \)

Solution for Q1

The gradient is calculated as:

\( f_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (y - x) = -1 \)
\( f_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (y - x) = 1 \)
Therefore, \( \nabla f = (-1, 1) \) at the point \( (2, 1) \).

So, the correct option is the first one.

Q2. Find the gradient of the function \( f(x, y) = \ln(x^2 + y^2) \) at the point \( (1, 1) \)

Solution for Q2

The gradient is calculated as:

\( f_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \ln(x^2 + y^2) = \frac{2x}{x^2 + y^2} \)
\( f_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \ln(x^2 + y^2) = \frac{2y}{x^2 + y^2} \)
At the point \( (1, 1) \), we have: \( \nabla f = \left(\frac{2(1)}{1^2 + 1^2}, \frac{2(1)}{1^2 + 1^2}\right) = \left(\frac{2}{2}, \frac{2}{2}\right) = (1, 1) \).

So, the correct option is the first one.

Q3. Find the gradient of the function \( g(x, y) = xy^2 \) at the point \( (2, -1) \)

Solution for Q3

The gradient is calculated as:

\( g_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (xy^2) = y^2 \)
\( g_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (xy^2) = 2xy \)
Therefore, \( \nabla g = (y^2, 2xy) \) at the point \( (2, -1) \).

So, the correct option is the first one.
