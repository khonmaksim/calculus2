QUIZ: Partial Derivatives

Q1. Find \( f_x \) and \( f_y \) for \( f(x, y) = 2x^2 - 3y - 4 \)

Solution for Q1

The partial derivatives are:

\( f_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (2x^2 - 3y - 4) = 4x \)
\( f_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (2x^2 - 3y - 4) = -3 \)

So, the correct option is the second one.

Q2. Find \( f_x \) and \( f_y \) for \( f(x, y) = (x^2 - 1)(y + 2) \)

Solution for Q2

The partial derivatives are:

\( f_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (x^2-1)(y+2) = 2x(y+2) \)
\( f_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (x^2-1)(y+2) = x^2-1 \)

So, the correct option is the first one.

Q3. Find \( f_x \), \( f_y \), and \( f_z \) for \( f(x, y, z) = 1 + xy^2 - 2z^2 \)

Solution for Q3

The partial derivatives are:

\( f_x = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(1 + xy^2 - 2z^2) = y^2 \)
\( f_y = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(1 + xy^2 - 2z^2) = 2xy \)
\( f_z = \frac{\partial}{\partial z}(1 + xy^2 - 2z^2) = -4z \)

So, the correct option is the first one.
